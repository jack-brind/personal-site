itris KPI Dashboard

Product design, Interaction design, User research
2024

Context

The recruitment industry is numbers-driven and KPIs play a critical role in measuring individual, team, and organisational performance. For recruiters, these metrics influence promotions, pay reviews, and commissions, making them a powerful motivator. For a business, these metrics indicate profitability and efficiency, among other things. By providing clarity and focus on targets, KPIs help individuals understand expectations, driving both personal and business success.

Problem

While some data was available in itris, it was scattered across different areas of the product, making it difficult to keep track of. There was also no way to record targets against any metrics, nor was there a dedicated space for users to monitor performance metrics efficiently. This oversight manifested in increased frustration and inefficiencies and as a result, users had to rely on workarounds, such as exporting multiple reports and manipulating them in spreadsheets, which was time-consuming and cumbersome.

This inefficiency and lack of target options on metrics had a direct business impact, contributing to churn, lost deals, and ultimately an estimated Â£1.4m risk to annual revenue.

Goals

User

  • Remove user frustration
  • Provide a streamlined, efficient and flexible way to track metrics
  • Users should be able to drill down into the data behind the metrics
  • Users should have a single place to visualise their KPIs

Business

  • Protect the risked revenue
  • Reduce the risk of customer churn
  • Remove barriers to sales conversions

Research

We had a good amount of feedback on this problem already which came primarily from speaking to customers and other feedback streams, but also from the sales team. In addition, I analysed some competitors as well as similar stand-alone tools in the market.

This provided a good amount of insight, but I had some knowledge gaps that I wanted to fill and assumptions to validate. So, I also conducted a survey to customers to dig a little deeper into some key themes identified initially. The results of the survey provided some clarity, including:

  • Nearly a third of respondents set collective KPIs for groups of users as opposed to individual users
  • Weekly KPIs were the most popular, with monthly KPIs a close second
  • The importance of being able to drill down into the KPI data was rated 92%
  • Most respondents were exporting reports to Excel to track KPIs

Key insight

Historically, we understood KPIs to be target-based, but I recognised an interesting pattern in the survey, and I was keen to pull on the thread. After following up with some respondents, my suspicions were confirmed that some KPIs were set without specific targets. This became a key insight, and one we wouldn't have considered in the initial scope without speaking to customers.

Solution

Once the research phase was complete, I collaborated with the development, product and QA teams to outline the initial scope for the project. The scope consisted of:

  • a total of 25 configurable metrics to track
  • granular configurability for nuanced metrics
  • historical tracking and auditing when targets are updated
  • a dashboard for users to keep track of their performance
  • drill down capabilities to dig deeper into the data behind the KPIs

KPIs would be configurable in the settings area and would be tied to a user, team, or the whole organisation (i.e. all users).

It was also crucial that KPIs were communicated to the user well. After many iterations of the KPI card, I settled on a final design that struck the right balance between information density and visual noise.

The KPI cards were then displayed in a dedicated dashboard for the user so that they could have a single source of truth for their individual performance as well as their contributions to collective team and organisational metrics.

Users can also drill down into the data behind the KPIs to see the activity that contributed to the metric. Users can export the data or convert it to a search.

Outcome

The feature shipped shortly before I left the company and so had not yet been available for long enough to generate measurable results. However, early internal feedback was very positive and as a result, the sales team felt more confident during customer demos, and stakeholders saw this as a key improvement given the potential revenue at risk. This work directly addressed known pain points and is expected to lead to more deal conversions and a lower risk to revenue.
